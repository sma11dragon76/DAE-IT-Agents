Wetstock Management Agent: Eliminating Fuel Loss

The DAE Wetstock Management AI agent delivers quantifiable and immediate financial returns by providing real-time, pinpoint accuracy in fuel volume reconciliation.

Current Pain Points:

  • Significant revenue loss from undetected inventory variance, fuel theft, meter drift, and temperature fluctuations.
  • Time-consuming, error-prone manual reconciliation processes.
  • Failure to pinpoint root causes of discrepancies, leading to millions in annual losses.

DAE AI Agent Solution & Outcomes:

  • Real-Time Inventory Visibility & Loss Tracking: Precisely tracks fuel loss and identifies unusual consumption patterns. (Outcome: Unparalleled visibility, detection of theft/irregularities).
  • Proven Financial Impact: Demonstrated 64% reduction in tank gauge calibration variances and 15% reduction in investigation efforts. (Outcome: Substantial savings).
  • Root Cause Identification & Automated Investigation: Identifies controllable (meter drift, theft) and uncontrollable (temperature) losses. (Outcome: Saved time and resources on manual discrepancy resolution).
  • Direct Profitability Boost: Plugs major revenue leakage and optimizes operational efficiency. (Outcome: Significant boost to retailer's bottom line).

An undetected variance of just 0.15% can lead to losses exceeding €10,000 annually per site.

Strategic Roadmap

AI Agent Development Prioritization

DAE will prioritize AI agent development based on least development effort, lower complexity, and higher probability of immediate revenue. This is visualized using a Value vs. Complexity approach.

Quick Wins / Immediate Impact

  • Wetstock Management Agent: Directly addresses quantifiable fuel loss. Proven ROI (e.g., 64% reduction in calibration variances). Relatively focused development.
  • Pricing Optimization Agent: Directly impacts profitability and retention. Significant revenue upside. Human-in-the-loop approach practical.

Strategic Value / Moderate Complexity

  • Finance Agent (Demand Forecasting): Foundational for Wetstock & Logistics. Reduces inventory costs (15-25%).
  • Asset Management Agent (Predictive Maintenance): Reduces unplanned downtime, improves customer experience. Requires IoT integration.

Longer-Term / Higher Complexity

  • Operations Agent: Improves customer experience. May involve complex computer vision.
  • Logistics Agent: Optimizes delivery. Requires real-time traffic/HAZMAT data.
  • Marketing Agent: Enhances loyalty. Deep customer behavior analysis.
  • IT Agent (Comprehensive): Foundational but less immediate direct revenue. Highly complex (securing AI itself).
  • HSSE Agent: Mitigates high-impact risks. Complex integration, high accuracy needed.

Technical Plan

A robust technical architecture is key. This includes a solid data foundation, a flexible integration framework, and scalable deployment.

1. Data Foundation & MLOps:

Leverage existing CMS. Implement data quality strategy (cleansing, governance). Adopt MLOps (e.g., ZenML, MLflow, Kubeflow) for model lifecycle management, crucial for a lean team. Use containerization (Docker, Kubernetes).

2. Integration Framework (Middleware):

Hub-and-Spoke or Publish-Subscribe model. API Gateways for secure access to third-party CMS. Support industry standards (IFSF, OPC UA, Conexxus).

3. Scalability & Deployment:

Microservices architecture for agility and fault isolation. Containerization (Docker) orchestrated by Kubernetes for efficient deployment, including edge computing scenarios.

Leveraging Singapore Government Grants

As a Singapore SME with >30% local ownership, DAE can access grants to minimize equity dilution. Most require SG registration, local shareholding, and financial viability.

Enterprise Development Grant (EDG)

Purpose: Supports Singapore companies in upgrading, innovating, and growing, including automation and technology development.

Funding: Up to 70% of qualifying costs (consultancy, software/equipment, internal manpower, training).

Relevance for DAE: Highly relevant for custom AI agent development, covering salaries and software/equipment costs.

Productivity Solutions Grant (PSG)

Purpose: Supports adoption of pre-approved IT solutions and equipment.

Funding: Up to 50% of eligible costs.

Relevance for DAE: Could be used for integrating "ready-made AI solutions" or tools (e.g., AI chatbots, inventory systems) into the platform, reducing custom development scope.

AI Singapore (AISG) 100E Programme

Purpose: Co-funds AI projects not solvable by off-the-shelf solutions, focusing on measurable outcomes. Provides AI expertise.

Funding: AISG contributes up to S$250,000 per project.

Relevance for DAE: Ideal for complex, custom AI agents (e.g., Pricing, IT Security, HSSE) requiring bespoke models. Directly supports core AI R&D.

Enterprise Compute Initiative

Purpose: New S$150M initiative to accelerate AI adoption and advanced computing for businesses, especially SMEs.

Funding/Support: Partnership with major cloud providers for AI tools, compute power, expert consultancy.

Relevance for DAE: Addresses high infrastructure/computational costs for AI development (e.g., GPU instances), significantly offsetting cloud expenses.

Enterprise Innovation Scheme (EIS)

Purpose: Encourages R&D and IP development.

Funding: 400% tax deductions on first S$400,000 of qualifying R&D expenses in SG. Option to convert up to S$100,000 expenditure into non-taxable cash payout.

Relevance for DAE: Powerful incentive for core AI development, drastically reducing effective R&D cost (staff, consumables, outsourced R&D).

Startup SG Tech

Purpose: Early-stage funding for tech startups to develop and commercialize groundbreaking proprietary technology.

Funding: Proof-of-Concept (POC) grants up to S$250,000; Proof-of-Viability (POV) grants up to S$500,000.

Relevance for DAE: If AI agents platform is a new venture/product line, provides crucial early capital for validating technical/commercial viability. Requires core R&D in SG.

Development Location Strategy

The choice of development location impacts cost, grant eligibility, and operations. A mixed model is recommended.

Location Model Pros Cons
China-Based (Shijiazhuang) Lower labor costs, Proximity to existing China customers, Large talent pool. Limited SG grant access, Data privacy/transfer complexities (PIPL), Higher corporate tax (25%).
Singapore-Based Maximized grant eligibility, Strong AI ecosystem & R&D support, Favorable IP protection, Lower corporate tax (17%). Higher labor costs, Higher cost of living, Competitive talent market.
Mixed Development Model (Recommended) Optimized cost-benefit, Grant eligibility (SG core R&D), Strategic oversight (SG), Mitigated risk. Increased management complexity, Cultural/communication nuances, Grant compliance nuances.

Recommendation: Anchor core AI R&D in Singapore (lean, high-caliber team) to leverage grants. Maintain larger, cost-effective development/QA team in China. Strategically allocate work based on grant eligibility.

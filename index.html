DAE Modular AI Agent Platform – Business & Technical Plan

Executive Summary

DAE is a leading provider of integrated hardware and software solutions for energy retail stations across APAC and China. Our Forecourt Controller (FCC), Central Management System (CMS), and POS solutions are deployed at over 20 customer sites, enabling seamless management of dispensers, EV chargers, hydrogen pumps, ATG, CCTV, and more. With access to comprehensive station-level data through our CMS, DAE aims to launch a suite of modular AI Agents tailored to replicate or enhance key operational roles within energy retail businesses. These AI Agents will be modular, integrable with third-party CMS platforms, and commercially available to energy providers in APAC and the Middle East.

Business Objectives

  • Enhance or replace existing roles in energy retail businesses using AI Agents.
  • Offer modular AI functionality tailored to specific roles like Finance, Operations, HSSE, Wetstock, Logistics, Marketing, Pricing, and IT.
  • Ensure interoperability with third-party CMS platforms via robust API ingestion and transformation layers.
  • Commercialize AI Agents through our existing CMS platform or as add-ons to competitors' platforms.

Market Opportunity

The energy retail sector is undergoing rapid digital transformation. With increasing complexity in managing fuel, EV, hydrogen, and other energy types, AI can drive major improvements in efficiency, compliance, and profitability. Modular AI Agents offer a scalable, non-disruptive path to automation, meeting immediate and evolving needs of operators across APAC and the Middle East.

Product Architecture

Core Components

  • Forecourt Controller (FCC): Connects and manages all onsite hardware devices.
  • Central Management System (CMS): Aggregates data from FCCs and POS across the network.
  • AI Agent Platform: Pluggable AI modules integrated with CMS or external APIs.
  • Third-party CMS Integration Layer: API and ETL-based data ingestion pipeline to normalize external CMS data for AI use.

AI Agents Overview

  • Finance Agent: Tracks sales, revenue, fuel cost trends, and generates financial forecasts.
  • Operations Agent: Ensures station operational continuity and flags disruptions.
  • HSSE Agent: Monitors compliance, incident detection, and predictive safety analytics.
  • Wetstock Agent: Reconciles fuel volumes and detects potential leaks or theft.
  • Logistics Agent: Coordinates fuel deliveries and optimizes route planning.
  • Marketing Agent: Suggests campaigns and customer retention strategies.
  • Pricing Agent: Uses competitor and demand data to recommend optimal prices.
  • IT Agent: Enforces data security, access control, and system compliance.

Third-party CMS Integration

The platform includes an integration layer that enables seamless data exchange from third-party CMS systems. This includes:

  • Custom APIs and webhooks for real-time or batch data ingestion.
  • AI-powered ETL agents that clean, standardize, and format data into schemas compatible with DAE’s AI Agents.
  • Metadata tagging and normalization pipelines to ensure consistent semantic context across data sources.

Technical Rollout Plan

  1. Launch core AI Agent SDK embedded in existing DAE CMS.
  2. Build and release integration layer for 3rd-party CMS compatibility.
  3. Develop role-specific Agents as microservices accessible via REST APIs.
  4. Deploy customer portal for managing and subscribing to desired AI modules.
  5. Release monitoring dashboards and explainability features for agent outputs.

Commercial Strategy

  • Offer agents as modular subscriptions bundled with DAE CMS or standalone via API integration.
  • Partner with energy solution providers in APAC and the Middle East to integrate into existing ecosystems.
  • Leverage DAE’s reputation and existing client base to seed adoption.
  • Use a freemium model for one AI Agent (e.g., Wetstock or Pricing) to drive initial interest.

Conclusion

DAE's Modular AI Agent Platform aims to transform the energy retail sector by embedding intelligence into every layer of station operations. Through native integration with our CMS and compatibility with third-party systems, we offer a scalable path toward full automation and enhanced decision-making for our clients across the globe.
